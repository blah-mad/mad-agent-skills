# SaaS GTM Strategy

Use this to turn GTM ambiguity into a small, executable plan. Avoid MBA-generic advice; make a call, name assumptions, and define the test.

## Workflow

1. Snapshot the context.
   - Product category, stage, core job, sales motion, budget/team constraints.
   - ICP, buyer, champion, user, evaluator, approver.
   - Pain severity, urgency, current alternatives, switching trigger.
   - Proof available: customers, usage, case studies, security, benchmarks, integrations, demos, founder credibility.

2. Pick the beachhead.
   - Rank segments by pain, reachability, budget, sales cycle, product fit, and expansion.
   - Name the first buying moment.
   - Name disqualifiers so the team does not chase every adjacent audience.

3. Write the positioning.
   - Category frame: what it is and what it replaces or complements.
   - Promise: business outcome.
   - Mechanism: believable reason it works.
   - Proof: evidence, not adjectives.
   - Objections: why now, why switch, why trust, why pay.
   - CTA: next action for the sales motion.

4. Shape the offer.
   - Entry offer: trial, demo, pilot, design partner, free tool, paid proof of concept, starter plan.
   - Pricing logic: seat, usage, workspace, volume, outcome, platform fee, or hybrid.
   - Expansion path: users, usage, teams, workflows, compliance, support, premium capability.

5. Choose channels.
   - Use channels that match the buying moment: founder-led outbound, warm network, SEO/content, templates, ecosystem/marketplace, community, events, partners, paid.
   - For each channel: audience, offer, asset, weekly activity, metric, stop/continue threshold.

6. Produce the 90-day plan.
   - One positioning or offer test at a time.
   - Weekly actions, assets, owners, sales enablement, feedback loop, dashboard.
   - Keep the plan small enough for the actual team.

## Output Shape

```text
Recommendation: <GTM move>
ICP: <beachhead and disqualifiers>
Positioning: <frame/promise/mechanism/proof/CTA>
Offer: <entry/pricing/expansion>
Channels: <plan with thresholds>
90 days: <weekly execution>
Metrics: <dashboard>
Assumptions/tests: <what must be validated>
```
